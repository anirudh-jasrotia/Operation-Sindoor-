Why Operation Sindoor Started

On April 22, 2025, a brutal terrorist attack took place in Baisaran Valley, near Pahalgam in Jammu and Kashmir. The attackers killed 26 people ‚Äî 25 Hindu tourists from other parts of India and 1 local Muslim.

In response to this brutal attack, the Indian government launched Operation Sindoor on May 6, 2025. The operation involved precision airstrikes targeting nine sites in Pakistan and Pakistan-administered Kashmir, which India identified as terrorist infrastructure linked to groups like Jaish-e-Mohammed and Lashkar-e-Taiba. The name "Sindoor" symbolizes the vermilion mark worn by married Hindu women, reflecting the operation's dedication to the victims, many of whom were Hindu men, leaving their wives widowed.

Key Events of Operation Sindoor

  • May 7, 2025: India launched Operation Sindoor, destroying 9 terrorist camps in Pakistan and Pakistan-occupied Kashmir through precision missile strikes, killing over 100 militants.
  • May 8, 2025: Pakistan responded with drone and missile attacks on Indian cities, while India successfully intercepted many using its S-400 air defense system.
  • May 9, 2025: The fighting between India and Pakistan became more intense. Intense cross-border shelling and drone attacks occurred as Pakistan targeted 26 Indian locations with armed drones, injuring civilians. 26 locations, including Baramulla, Kupwara, Poonch, Uri, Samba, Kathua, Jammu, Akhnoor, Firozpur, Amritsar, Pathankot, Chandigarh, Patiala, Jalandhar, Ludhiana, Ambala, Hisar, Jaipur, Alwar, Bharatpur, Agra, Mathura, Delhi, Ghaziabad, Noida, Greater Noida, and Jaipur.

Current Status (22 April to 10 May)

Operation Sindoor has entered a critical phase. Indian forces have secured several key infiltration routes and destroyed multiple terror bunkers. Border villages have been evacuated for safety, and relief camps have been set up. Pakistan continues to deny involvement, but India maintains high surveillance.

Global Impact:

1.Global Security Concerns:

The India-Pakistan conflict is a nuclear standoff between two nations with nuclear weapons. Any escalation in hostilities could risk a nuclear conflict, with catastrophic consequences for not just the countries involved, but the entire world. Countries with nuclear capabilities, such as China, the United States, Russia, and others, are closely monitoring the situation.

2.Impact on Trade and Global Economy:

India and Pakistan are crucial players in the South Asian economy. A prolonged conflict could disrupt trade routes in the region, especially through the Chabahar Port and Gwadar Port, potentially affecting the global supply chain. If the conflict escalates, international markets, particularly those in Asia and the Middle East, could face economic instability, with increased oil prices and supply shortages.

3.Humanitarian Crisis:

The conflict has already led to the displacement of thousands of civilians, with refugees fleeing across borders, primarily to India. The humanitarian situation would worsen with more casualties, further exacerbating the refugee crisis in neighboring countries, such as Afghanistan, and potentially even causing a crisis in international law regarding human rights and refugee protection.

4.Increased Global Tensions:

Countries such as the United States, China, Russia, and European Union have interests in both India and Pakistan. A conflict in the region could influence diplomatic relationships worldwide, forcing nations to pick sides or, in some cases, increase their military presence in the region. Countries with strategic interests in the Indian Ocean, such as Iran and Saudi Arabia, would also be impacted.

5.Terrorism and Extremism:

A conflict could fuel terrorism and extremism in other parts of the world, as non-state actors may see this as an opportunity to disrupt other regions. Additionally, militant groups like Al-Qaeda, ISIS, and others could find fertile ground to further their agendas in the instability caused by the war.

6.Diplomatic Repercussions:

The international community, especially bodies like the United Nations and regional powers like China and the U.S., would play a crucial role in peacekeeping. A conflict could strain existing diplomatic relationships and lead to changes in international alliances, creating a ripple effect that could affect global governance structures.

7.Environmental Impact:

War between two nuclear-armed nations could have environmental consequences, especially if nuclear weapons are used. The "nuclear winter" theory suggests that a nuclear exchange could disrupt global climate patterns, leading to a drop in temperatures, destruction of the ozone layer, and widespread agricultural failure, affecting food security worldwide.

In summary, the India-Pakistan war has far-reaching global effects ranging from security concerns to economic impacts, humanitarian crises, and diplomatic strains, which could alter the balance of global power and security for the foreseeable future.
